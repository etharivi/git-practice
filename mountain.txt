Mountain

Mountains are large landforms that rise prominently above their surroundings,
typically featuring steep slopes and a high peak.

Formation
Formed by tectonic forces or volcanism pushing the earth's crust upward over
millions of years.

Notable Examples
- Mount Everest — highest above sea level
- K2 — second highest, notoriously difficult to climb
- The Himalayas — the youngest and tallest mountain range

Life at Altitude
Cold temperatures, thin air, and hardy species like snow leopards, ibex, and
alpine plants that survive harsh conditions.
