Forest

A forest is a large area dominated by trees, forming a complex ecosystem that
supports countless species.

Types of Forests
- Tropical rainforests — warm, wet, and highly biodiverse
- Temperate forests — distinct seasons, mixed trees
- Boreal (taiga) — cold, coniferous, northern

The Green Lungs
Forests absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, playing a vital role in
regulating the planet's climate.

Biodiversity
From towering canopy trees to insects on the forest floor, forests host more than
half of the world's land species.
