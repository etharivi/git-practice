Backwater

Backwaters are a network of brackish lagoons, lakes, and canals lying parallel to
a coast, where river water meets the sea.

How They Form
Formed by the action of waves and shore currents creating low barrier islands and
enclosed bodies of water.

Kerala Backwaters
India's famous backwaters stretch over 900 km, navigated by traditional
houseboats known as kettuvallam.

Ecosystem
A unique mix of freshwater and saltwater species — fish, crabs, waterbirds, and
lush palm-lined banks.
